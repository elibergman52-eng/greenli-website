The Hard Rock Opportunity

A waste stream that the industry largely ignores.

Spodumene refiners — the processors who convert hard rock lithium ore into battery-grade material — generate large volumes of lithium-rich wastewater as a byproduct of the refining process. At concentrations exceeding 30,000 ppm, this stream carries enormous lithium value.

Despite this, the industry captures an estimated 4% of that lithium. The remainder is discharged as tailings — a financial loss and, in many jurisdictions, a growing environmental liability as discharge standards tighten.

M.L.E. is a bolt-on solution. It integrates with the wastewater handling infrastructure already in place, requires no modification to upstream refining lines, and converts the discharge stream into a revenue-generating output.

What M.L.E. Does Here

Turn a disposal cost into a margin line.

Convert a waste stream into a revenue stream

Recovered lithium from wastewater is purified to battery-grade quality. A new saleable output with no change to upstream refining operations.

Reduce environmental liability on tailings discharge

De-lithiated effluent after M.L.E. processing has materially lower lithium concentration, reducing environmental impact and reducing future regulatory exposure.

Improve total lithium recovery per tonne of ore processed

Every tonne of ore already mined, transported, and refined. M.L.E. captures value that is already paid for — with no incremental extraction cost.

Optional D.C.M. integration from year three

At sufficient scale, the recovered lithium permeate can feed directly into greenLi's Direct Cathode Manufacture system — producing battery-ready cathode material on site and capturing additional supply-chain margin.

Relevant Operators

Spodumene refiners including Talison, Covalent, Pilbara Minerals, Core Lithium, and peers processing hard rock lithium ore in Australia and beyond.
