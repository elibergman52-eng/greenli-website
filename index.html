Why Throughput Matters

High flow changes the math.

Conventional Direct Lithium Extraction (D.L.E.) is concentration-bound. It works on rich brines and struggles below 200 ppm. Membrane Lithium Extraction (M.L.E.) is throughput-bound. We process larger volumes continuously, which means meaningful recovery is viable at concentrations and temperatures where conventional extraction is not.

That difference opens up entire categories of assets: produced water from oil and gas, geothermal return streams at 60 degrees Celsius, groundwater at 20 ppm. Assets the industry has written off.

Conventional D.L.E.

Concentration-bound. Requires >200 ppm. Below 40°C. Fails on low-concentration or high-temperature feeds.

M.L.E.

Throughput-bound. Operates at 20 ppm. Up to 80°C. Viable on produced water, geothermal brine, and low-concentration groundwater.

Beyond Extraction

From brine to cathode, in one platform.

Most lithium asset operators stop at carbonate or hydroxide. They sell into a refining market where margins compress and dependencies sit outside their control.

Direct Cathode Manufacture (D.C.M.) is different. It converts purified lithium directly into battery-ready cathode powder on site. No intermediate salts, no transport to a refiner, no cathode chemistry lock-in. Operators capture the margin that today flows to cell manufacturers.
